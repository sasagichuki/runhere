LET'S RUN TO SAVE THE MAU FOREST

Fewer trees mean less water.

The Mau forest is the largest indigenous mountain forest in East Africa, and the largest water catchment area in Kenya. Decades of deforestation and environmental degradation led to a decline in the quantity of water. Approximately 10 million people and countless wildlife species depend on the rivers fed by the forest Complex, upto 60% of lake Victoria's water flows from the Forest Complex.
